Machine Learning-enabled feedback loops for metal powder bed fusion additive manufacturing
Publication Details
Paper Title Machine Learning-enabled feedback loops for metal powder bed fusion additive manufacturing
Authors Chao Liu, Léopold Le Roux, Ze Ji, Pierre Kerfriden, Franck Lacan, Samuel Bigot
Journal Procedia Computer Science
Publication Info Volume 176, 2020, Pages 2586-2595
Publisher Elsevier
DOI 10.1016/j.procs.2020.09.314
Abstract
Metal Powder Bed Fusion (PBF) has been attracting an increasing attention as an emerging metal Additive Manufacturing (AM) technology. Despite its distinctive advantages compared to traditional subtractive manufacturing such as high design flexibility, short development time, low tooling cost, and low production waste, the inconsistent part quality caused by inappropriate product design, non-optimal process plan and inadequate process control has significantly hindered its wide acceptance in the industry.
Research Overview
To improve the part quality control in metal PBF process, this paper proposes a novel Machine Learning (ML)-enabled approach for developing feedback loops throughout the entire metal PBF process. The research addresses the critical challenge of inconsistent part quality in metal additive manufacturing by developing intelligent feedback control systems.
The paper presents a categorisation of metal PBF feedback loops along with a summary of the critical PBF manufacturing data in each process stage. A generic framework of ML-enabled metal PBF feedback loops is developed to enable real-time process monitoring and control, addressing the fundamental challenges that have hindered the wide industrial acceptance of metal powder bed fusion technology.
This foundational work establishes the conceptual and technical frameworks for implementing machine learning-driven feedback control systems in additive manufacturing, enabling automated process optimization and quality control throughout the entire manufacturing chain.
Significance and Impact
This research represents foundational work in applying machine learning to create intelligent feedback control systems for metal additive manufacturing. By addressing the inconsistent quality issues that have hindered industrial adoption of metal PBF technology, the work provides practical frameworks for implementing ML-enabled process control.
The proposed approach enables manufacturers to leverage the distinctive advantages of additive manufacturing - high design flexibility, short development time, low tooling cost, and low production waste - while overcoming quality control challenges through intelligent feedback systems that can adapt and optimize manufacturing processes in real-time.
