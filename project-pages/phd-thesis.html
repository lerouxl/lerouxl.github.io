PhD Thesis - Machine learning techniques for the optimisation and simulation of metal additive layer manufacturing process chains
PhD thesis on machine learning techniques for optimizing and simulating metal additive manufacturing process chains, bridging the gap between computational efficiency and manufacturing precision.
Thesis Overview
My PhD thesis, "Machine Learning Techniques for the Optimisation and Simulation of Metal Additive Layer Manufacturing Process Chains," represents the culmination of five years of intensive research at Cardiff University (2019-2024). This comprehensive work addresses critical challenges in metal additive manufacturing by developing novel machine learning approaches that dramatically improve computational efficiency while maintaining manufacturing precision.
The research tackles fundamental problems in industrial manufacturing where traditional simulation methods are prohibitively expensive and time-consuming. By leveraging cutting-edge machine learning techniques, particularly Graph Neural Networks and Convolutional Neural Networks, the work demonstrates how AI can revolutionize manufacturing process optimization and quality control.
Key Research Contributions
Graph Neural Network Innovation: Developed novel GNN architectures specifically designed for metal additive manufacturing deformation prediction, achieving 10x speedup compared to traditional finite element methods while maintaining comparable accuracy levels.
CNN-based Defect Detection: Created sophisticated computer vision systems for automated quality assessment in layer-by-layer manufacturing processes, achieving 96% precision in defect identification and significantly reducing manual inspection requirements.
Simulation Acceleration: Demonstrated how machine learning can serve as effective surrogate models for complex physical simulations, enabling real-time process optimization that was previously computationally intractable.
Industrial Integration: Developed practical frameworks for integrating ML techniques into existing manufacturing workflows, ensuring research outcomes could be translated into real-world industrial applications.
Methodological Approach
Multi-Scale Modeling: Employed a comprehensive approach that addressed manufacturing challenges at multiple scales, from micro-scale defect detection to macro-scale process optimization.
Data-Driven Insights: Leveraged large-scale simulation datasets and experimental data to train robust machine learning models that could generalize across different manufacturing conditions and materials.
Validation Framework: Established rigorous validation protocols that ensured ML models maintained physical consistency and could be trusted for industrial deployment.
Reproducible Research: Implemented comprehensive documentation and open-source tool development to ensure research reproducibility and enable broader community adoption.
Research Impact & Significance
Academic Contribution: The thesis advances the state-of-the-art in applying machine learning to manufacturing applications, providing foundational work for future research in AI-driven manufacturing optimization.
Industrial Relevance: Research outcomes have direct applications in improving manufacturing efficiency, reducing costs, and enhancing quality control in metal additive manufacturing processes.
Interdisciplinary Bridge: Successfully bridged the gap between advanced machine learning research and practical manufacturing engineering, demonstrating the value of interdisciplinary collaboration.
Future Directions: Established frameworks and methodologies that can be extended to other manufacturing domains and advanced AI techniques, providing a foundation for continued research and development.
Technical Achievements
Publication Record: The research resulted in multiple peer-reviewed publications in prestigious venues including Procedia CIRP and Journal of Manufacturing Systems, contributing to the scientific literature.
Open Source Contribution: Developed and published open-source tools for simulation data extraction and processing, enabling broader community access to research methodologies.
Industry Collaboration: Worked closely with industrial partners to ensure research outcomes were practically relevant and could be implemented in real manufacturing environments.
Computational Innovation: Demonstrated expertise in high-performance computing, managing complex model training on supercomputer infrastructure with multi-GPU configurations.
