EPSYL - Data Scientist (Simulation & Optimization)
Bridging the gap between complex industrial simulations and practical optimization through advanced surrogate modeling, delivering significant performance improvements for industrial refrigeration systems.
Role Overview
At EPSYL (2023–2024), I worked as a Data Scientist specializing in Simulation & Optimization, where I developed sophisticated surrogate models to accelerate and optimize industrial processes. My role focused on creating machine learning solutions that could effectively replace time-consuming simulations while maintaining accuracy.
The position required deep understanding of both simulation workflows and advanced machine learning techniques, particularly Gaussian Process regression. I collaborated closely with simulation engineers and process optimization teams to translate complex industrial challenges into tractable ML problems.
Key Achievements
Surrogate Model Development: Designed and implemented surrogate models using Gaussian Process regression with scikit-learn to emulate complex simulation workflows, enabling rapid process optimization without running expensive simulations.
Industrial Impact: Delivered +20% improvement in process KPIs for industrial refrigeration systems by optimizing operating parameters through intelligent surrogate modeling and optimization algorithms.
Data Pipeline Management: Created comprehensive data curation and preprocessing pipelines for simulation training datasets, ensuring high-quality inputs for machine learning models and maintaining data integrity throughout the workflow.
Model Lifecycle Management: Managed complete model development lifecycle including training, evaluation, and model selection processes on industrial datasets, implementing robust validation frameworks for production deployment.
Optimization & Design: Implemented geometry optimization using Monte Carlo Markov Chain (MCMC) methods and coordinated Design of Experiments (DOE) activities with simulation engineers to maximize information gain from limited computational resources.
Technical Approach
Surrogate Modeling: Leveraged Gaussian Process regression to create fast, accurate emulations of CFD and thermal simulation workflows, enabling real-time optimization of industrial processes that previously required hours of simulation time.
Optimization Algorithms: Implemented advanced optimization strategies including MCMC-based parameter space exploration and adaptive sampling techniques to efficiently navigate complex design spaces.
Industrial Collaboration: Worked closely with domain experts to understand physical constraints and process requirements, ensuring ML solutions were both technically sound and practically implementable in industrial settings.
Performance Validation: Established rigorous validation protocols to ensure surrogate models maintained accuracy across the full range of operating conditions, with particular attention to edge cases and process boundaries.
Technical Stack
Machine Learning: Gaussian Process regression, scikit-learn, MCMC optimization, Design of Experiments (DOE), statistical modeling
Simulation Integration: CFD workflow automation, thermal simulation coupling, industrial process modeling
Data Management: Automated preprocessing pipelines, simulation dataset curation, model training and evaluation frameworks
Industrial Applications: Refrigeration system optimization, process parameter tuning, performance KPI improvement
