Research Overview
During my PhD at Cardiff University (2019–2024), I conducted cutting-edge research in "Machine Learning Techniques for Metal Additive Manufacturing Process Chains." My work focused on developing novel AI approaches to accelerate and optimize complex manufacturing simulations, bridging the gap between computational efficiency and manufacturing precision.
The research addressed critical challenges in metal additive manufacturing, where traditional simulation methods are computationally expensive and time-consuming. By leveraging advanced machine learning techniques, I developed solutions that maintained accuracy while dramatically reducing computational requirements, enabling real-time process optimization.
Key Research Achievements
Graph Neural Network Innovation: Developed novel Graph Neural Network (GNN) architectures using PyTorch Geometric to predict deformation in metal additive manufacturing processes, achieving 10x speedup compared to traditional finite element methods while maintaining comparable accuracy.
CNN Defect Detection: Built and deployed Convolutional Neural Network models for automated defect detection in manufacturing processes, achieving 96% precision in identifying critical defects and significantly reducing manual inspection requirements.
High-Performance Computing: Managed large-scale model training on ARCCA supercomputer infrastructure, utilizing multi-GPU setups for training sessions lasting several days, demonstrating expertise in distributed computing and resource optimization.
MLOps Implementation: Developed comprehensive monitoring and tracking pipelines using Weights & Biases, enabling reproducible research and efficient hyperparameter optimization across multiple model architectures.
Open Source Contribution: Published open-source tools for Simufact data extraction, contributing to the broader research community and enabling other researchers to leverage similar datasets and methodologies.
Research Impact & Collaboration
Academic Publications: Authored multiple peer-reviewed publications in high-impact venues including Procedia CIRP and Journal of Manufacturing Systems, contributing to the scientific understanding of ML applications in manufacturing.
Industry Collaboration: Worked closely with industrial partners across France and the UK, ensuring research outcomes were practically relevant and could be translated into real-world manufacturing improvements.
Demonstrator Deployment: Successfully deployed CNN defect detection models in the project's industrial demonstrator, showcasing the transition from research prototype to production-ready solution.
Knowledge Transfer: Delivered reproducible AI solutions that could be adopted by industrial partners, emphasizing the importance of practical implementation alongside academic rigor.
