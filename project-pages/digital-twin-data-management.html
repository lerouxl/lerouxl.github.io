Digital twin-enabled collaborative data management for metal additive manufacturing systems
Publication Details
Paper Title Digital Twin-enabled Collaborative Data Management for Metal Additive Manufacturing Systems
Authors Chao Liu, Léopold Le Roux, Carolin Körner, Olivier Tabaste, Franck Lacan, Samuel Bigot
Journal Journal of Manufacturing Systems
Publication Info Volume 62, January 2022, Pages 857-874
DOI 10.1016/j.jmsy.2020.05.010
Research Overview
This research addresses the critical challenge of data management in metal additive manufacturing (AM) systems, where complex relationships between material properties, product design, process parameters, and product quality significantly impact industrial acceptance. The work proposes a novel Digital Twin-enabled collaborative data management framework comprising a Cloud DT that communicates with distributed Edge DTs across different product lifecycle stages.
The framework enables efficient implementation of advanced data analytics for intelligent process monitoring, control, and optimization in metal AM systems. A comprehensive metal AM product data model was developed to support collaborative data management, and the approach was validated through practical implementation in the European Union's H2020 MANUELA project involving 20 partner organizations.
Key Contributions
Digital Twin Architecture: Developed a novel Cloud DT and Edge DT architecture where the Cloud DT manages all product lifecycle data while Edge DTs focus on specific lifecycle stages with local computing capabilities for real-time processing and decision-making.
Comprehensive Data Model: Created a metal AM product data model containing critical data across five categories: design parameters, process parameters, process signatures, post-processing parameters, and product quality, supporting collaborative data management and advanced analytics.
Practical Implementation: Validated the framework through implementation in the MANUELA metal AM system, demonstrating feasibility across distributed manufacturing environments in multiple European countries.
ML-Enabled Applications: Demonstrated cloud-based and deep learning-enabled metal AM layer defect analysis, achieving 95% test accuracy using convolutional neural networks for real-time quality monitoring.
Technical Framework
System Architecture: The framework comprises six modules including a cloud-based collaborative data management platform and five product lifecycle stages (product design, process planning, manufacturing, post-processing, and quality measurement).
Edge Computing Integration: Edge DTs perform local real-time data processing tasks including machine control, process monitoring, in-process optimization, and simulation, while transferring processed data to the Cloud DT to reduce data traffic.
Data Management Strategy: Implemented using MSC MaterialCenter for flexible GUI development, database management, and external software integration, enabling secure cloud-based collaborative data management with different user access rights.
Interoperability: Supports various data formats including CAD files, sensor signals, images, and measurement reports, with XML schema-based data mapping for efficient bulk data upload and management.
MANUELA Project Implementation
International Collaboration: Implemented within the European Union's H2020 MANUELA project involving 20 partner organizations from research institutions and industrial companies across multiple European countries, demonstrating successful international collaboration.
Distributed Manufacturing: The system integrates three metal AM machines (in-house developed EBM machine, EOS M290 DMLS, and EOS M270 DMLS) equipped with different sensors and monitoring systems across geographically distributed shop floors.
Full Lifecycle Coverage: Encompasses the complete AM development cycle including simulation, robust AM manufacturing, online process control, characterization, real-time feedback, post-treatment, and AM qualification protocols.
Advanced Analytics: Integrated deep learning capabilities for layer defect analysis using over 16,000 electron optical images, demonstrating the framework's capability to enable sophisticated data-driven manufacturing applications.
Results & Impact
Enhanced Process Understanding: The framework enables fundamental understanding of metal AM processes through comprehensive data collection and analysis across all product lifecycle stages, supporting the development of simulation and prediction models.
Quality Improvement: Demonstrated significant potential for improving product quality and production efficiency through real-time layer defect detection and process optimization based on collaborative data management.
Industry 4.0 Advancement: Contributes to the advancement of Industry 4.0 and smart manufacturing by providing practical frameworks for implementing digital twin technologies in real manufacturing environments.
Standards Development: The comprehensive data model and collaborative framework contribute to the development of standards and best practices for digital twin implementation in manufacturing, influencing future research and industrial adoption.
